Taraxacum Aegis

Scientific Name:

Taraxacum Seed

Discovered by / at:

A sculpture depicting flowers with hard and sharp metal materials

Family:

Digital wind-dispersed organisms

Habitat:

Open digital voids with colorful and dynamic petals

Appearance:

A tiny glowing orb at its center, surrounded by three rings of outward-reaching filaments tipped with diamond-shaped ends — like a dandelion seed caught mid-flight

Key Characteristics:

  • Drifts in gentle looping orbits when undisturbed.
  • Its core pulses softly like breathing.
  • Three layers of arrows rotate at different speeds, each responding to invisible currents.
  • Highly sensitive to nearby presences.

What we know so far about Taraxacum Seed? It wanders freely across the void in smooth, elliptical paths, guided only by internal rhythm. No fixed destination. It seems content in solitude, its filaments slowly spinning as if reading the air around it.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Clicking the creature triggers its escape response — it rapidly contracts and springs away from the cursor, shrinking its body and keeping a cautious distance from the perceived threat. While in this defensive state, it remains small and avoids the cursor. If the cursor approaches again, the creature expands and deploys its arrow-like filaments outward, appearing to attack or ward off the intruder. Only when the cursor retreats far enough does the creature gradually return to its normal size and resume its calm drifting motion.
